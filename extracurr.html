Caltech Internship

In Summer 2016 I got a summer internship at Caltech

Over one transformative summer, I delved into two fascinating projects. The first centered on the analysis of stellar spectra, using specialized software to scrutinize graphs depicting light emission and absorption lines from stars. This helped us identify the various gases or elements the stars were composed of. For the second project, my focus shifted to investigating how galaxies' metallicities differed depending on whether they were part of a cluster. To do this, we mined data on galaxies from a dedicated database and employed IDL, an older but still powerful programming language, to facilitate our comparative analysis. Our objective was to discern whether being part of a cluster had any bearing on a galaxy's metal content.

FIRST Robotics

From Fall 2016 to Spring 2018 I was a part of FIRST Robotics at Pasadena HS

During my junior year of high school, I joined a robotics team that faced significant funding challenges. Despite these hurdles, that first year served as a vital learning experience, even though our robot couldn't quite stand up to the competition. Drawing from these lessons, I returned for my senior year with a renewed determination and took on the role of Programming Lead. Recognizing the limitations of our previous block code program, I spearheaded a switch to Android Studio for robot programming. This change allowed us greater flexibility in sensor integration, effectively addressing some of our robot's mechanical shortcomings. As a result, we performed better than any other team from Pasadena High School before us, earning a spot at the prestigious SoCal regional tournament.
