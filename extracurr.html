In the summer of 2015 I got a summer job at a place named Rapid Credit Reports

Here I was tasked with using html and css in order to make templates that could be sent out as an advertisement. I also was introduced to wordpress to make websites which I had never seen as I had only worked on websites with html and css

Caltech Internship

In the summer of 2016 I got a summer internship at Caltech

Here I worked on 2 projects over the summer. The first project required us to look at stellar spectra which was a graph that showed light coming from a star and it showed absorption lines which allowed us to see what gases or chemicals were coming from that star, in order to achieve this we used a program that allowed us to analyze the graph. The second project I worked on was to determine whether galaxies in clusters differed from lone galaxies when it came to their metallicity, in order to achieve this we collected information from galaxies in a database and wrote a program in an old language named IDL to help us compare them in order to determine if them being in a cluster affected their metallicity or not.

FIRST Robotics

From Fall 2016 to Spring 2018 I was a part of FIRST Robotics at Pasadena HS

In high school as a junior I joined a team that had no funding. The first year we didn't get to far as the robot we had made was not competitive enough. From that first year I learned a lot and it helped me to be better for my senior year. In my senior season I became the programming lead and decided that we needed to switch to andrioid studio to program the robots as the previous lead was using a block code program that I felt limited us greatly. With the android studio it was much easier to use sensors to help the robot with it's mechanical issues and in my senior season we qualified higher that any other team had at Pasadena High School by getting to the SoCal regional tournament.

Vex Robotics

From Fall 2018 to now I have been a part of VEX Robotics at CSUN

In CSUN VEX I have gained more experience coding, using sensors to get information from the environment in order to decide on how to complete tasks, working on autonomous robot routines, debugging code, leading people, understanding the mechanical side of robotics like gear ratios, stresses, CAD, and more. At CSUN robotics I have gotten many individual awards including rookie of the year, member of the year, and dedication awards but I have also helped lead my team to many team awards at competitions like tournament champion, tournament finalists, skills champion, excellence award, and design awards. I was the treasurer of the club last year and am currently the president of the club.
